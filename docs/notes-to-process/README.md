## Why This Place

The coding agent ecosystem is in a Cambrian explosion. Skills, agents, hooks, and extensions are proliferating across Claude Code, Cursor, Cline, and others. The only quality signals? Downloads and stars.

Popularity measures virality, not correctness. A skill that sounds confident will outperform one that's actually grounded in how LLMs process instructions, because most people can't tell the difference by reading the markdown. Platforms like skills.sh and Context7 solve discovery but not curation â€” they're search engines, not quality gates.

The Grand Bazaar exists to raise the bar. Not by gatekeeping, but by demonstrating a different approach:

- Research sources are documented. You can check our work.
- Design decisions are recorded. You can understand why we made the choices we did.
- Opinions are traceable to evidence. When we take a stance, we show what informed it.
- The process is visible end-to-end. You can reproduce it, review it, amend it.

That's the alternative to trust: transparency.
